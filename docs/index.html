Energy & Supply Chain (PIR 3)

The operation of data centers and AI training facilities is constrained by power and cooling. The 'Global Data Center Density and AI Compute Concentration' paper notes the enormous energy demands of modern data centers, particularly those supporting AI workloads. Generative AI requires massive deployments of GPUs, which demand massive power and space. This creates vulnerabilities tied to the reliability and security of power grids, water supplies for cooling, and supply chains for specialized hardware. The concentration of compute power in a few countries, as highlighted in the report, means that disruptions to these critical infrastructure components in those regions can have outsized global impacts. Kinetic vulnerabilities, such as substations and water treatment plants, become attractive targets for adversaries seeking to disrupt AI capabilities. The 'compute divide,' where only a small number of countries possess significant AI infrastructure, exacerbates these vulnerabilities because those nations are more vulnerable to targeted attacks.
